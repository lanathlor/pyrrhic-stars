# Dungeon Design

## Philosophy

Handcrafted dungeons. No procedural generation. Players should learn layouts by heart within 5 runs.

Dungeons are designed as modular environments assembled from asset kits. Each dungeon has a theme (derelict city, alien hive, orbital station, etc.) with a matching kit of 50-80 modular pieces.

Structure: linear-ish path with 3-4 boss encounters, trash packs between bosses, optional shortcuts or secrets.

Every encounter in a dungeon should test different things:

- Boss 1: teaches a core mechanic (simple telegraph, dodge timing)
- Boss 2: combines two mechanics (telegraph + add spawning)
- Boss 3: tests class-specific skills (FPS precision check, melee parry timing)
- Final boss: everything together with increasing pressure

## First Dungeon: Derelict City

Theme: Halo 3 ODST inspired. Night, rain, destroyed urban environment. Alien (Aceras) occupation.

Asset kit needed: street sections (straight, T, L, dead-end with rubble), building facades (8-10 variations), interior rooms (lobby, stairwell, collapsed office, underground parking), rubble/debris props, destroyed vehicles, street lights (broken), alien tech installations, barricades/containers.

Lighting: dark blue ambient, harsh alien spotlights, orange fire glow, volumetric fog, rain. Mood over polygon count.
