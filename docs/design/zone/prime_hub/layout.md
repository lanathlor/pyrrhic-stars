# Prime Hub — Zone Layout & Gameplay

## Overview

The Prime Hub is the player's home base — the social zone between combat missions. 500m x 500m, split across two vertical levels connected by a public lift.

This is where players form groups, pick loadouts, queue for dungeons, socialize, and eventually manage player housing. For Phase 0, it replaces the current minimal hub room.

---

## Upper Level — The Plaza

### Space Budget (~250m x 250m usable)

The upper level is an open-air rooftop district. Night sky overhead. The military building anchors one edge; the rest is gardens and walkways.

### Military Building

**Exterior**: A towering structure, **30+ stories tall**, dominating the plaza skyline. Clean, modern design — glass and white steel, Stark Tower vibes. NOT a military bunker. Think corporate tech HQ: welcoming, bright, business-professional. The Empire builds things that feel powerful but civilized — Apple Store meets Avengers Tower. The building is a landmark visible from anywhere on the upper level. Players enter at the ground-level entrance but the lift carries them to the **25th floor and above** — the operational floors. Lower floors are sealed (restricted access, lore justification: administrative offices, logistics, housing).

**Scale**: ~15-20m footprint, ~120m tall (30 floors at ~4m each). Players only access floors 25+. The exterior reads as a sleek glass tower; the interior is open, bright, floor-to-ceiling windows with city/sky views.

**Player-Accessible Floors (25th+)**:

-   **Floor 25 — Operations Lobby**: Where the lift arrives. Functional reception area. Holographic directory. Checkpoint gates (open in peacetime). The space channels players toward the upper floors.
-   **Floor 26 — Briefing & Portal**: Off the lobby corridor:
    -   **Briefing Room**: Mission board, dungeon selection, party loadout. Wall-mounted tactical displays.
    -   **Portal Room**: Dungeon launch point. Mana crystal gateway. This is where groups enter arenas.
-   **Floor 27+ — Future Content**: Armory/Workshop, Officer's Lounge, etc. For now: locked doors with red mana crystal indicators ("more content coming" without breaking immersion).
-   **The Lift**: Central feature connecting ground entrance to floor 25. Industrial design — large platform, metal cage, visible mana crystal drive mechanism. Long ride (~8-10 seconds) reinforcing the height.
-   **Locked Doors**: Several doors are visibly sealed (red mana crystal indicators). Restricted access is normal in a military building.

**Gameplay Purpose**: The military building is the **functional core** — everything a player needs to do between runs is here. Group up, gear up, queue up.

### Gardens

**Layout**: Wrapping around and between the military building and the plaza edge. Not a single flat park — varied terrain with gentle elevation changes, winding paths, open clearings, and denser planted sections.

**Key Features**:

-   **Central Clearing**: Open space for player gathering. Flat enough for emotes, showing off gear, group photos. A natural social magnet.
-   **Perimeter Walkways**: Along the plaza edge, with views of the night sky and the city below/around. Railings with integrated mana crystal lighting. Prime screenshot spots.
-   **Garden Alcoves**: Semi-private nooks off the main paths. Benches, small water features. Future: NPC vendors, seasonal event locations.
-   **The Lift Access Point**: A public lift platform at the plaza edge, clearly marked, leading down to the lower level. Separate from the military building's interior lift.

**Gameplay Purpose**: The gardens are the **social layer** — hanging out, exploring, taking in the view. Low-intensity space that rewards wandering.

### Traversal (Upper)

-   Players spawn near the military building entrance
-   Main paths radiate from the building to: garden central clearing, perimeter walkway, public lift
-   Walking time from spawn to any key point: under 30 seconds
-   No mounts, no sprint cooldowns — the upper level is intimate enough to walk

## Lower Level — The Streets

### Space Budget (~300m x 300m usable)

A dense network of streets and alleys anchored by a central plaza. Enclosed overhead — the upper level (or more city structure) is visible above, creating a canyon-like vertical feel.

### The Central Plaza

**Size**: ~80m x 60m. The largest open space on the lower level. This is the social and commercial heartbeat.

**Features**:

-   **Food stalls and vendor carts**: Arranged organically, not in a grid. Some permanent, some temporary-looking. Holographic menus, warm lighting, steam/smoke VFX from cooking.
-   **E-sport screens**: 2-3 large wall-mounted displays on surrounding buildings. Showing match replays, leaderboards, or ambient content. A natural gathering point.
-   **Seating clusters**: Mixed seating — benches, low tables, stacked crates repurposed as seats. Players and NPCs share the space.
-   **Central feature**: A fountain, monument, or public art installation. Something that gives the plaza a recognizable center point for navigation ("meet me at the fountain").

**Gameplay Purpose**: The social hub of the lower level. Player trading (future), LFG gathering, event staging area.

### The Streets

**Layout**: Organic street grid radiating from the central plaza. NOT a perfect grid — streets bend, fork, and dead-end. Think narrow European old-town streets scaled up with sci-fi infrastructure.

**Street Types**:

-   **Main arteries** (~8m wide): Connect the plaza to key destinations. Well-lit, clear signage. Players won't get lost on these.
-   **Side streets** (~4-5m wide): Shops, bars, small venues. More character, less traffic. Discoverable content lives here.
-   **Alleys** (~2-3m wide): Shortcuts, atmosphere. Dim lighting, graffiti, memorial shrines. Optional exploration, no critical content.

**Key Destinations Off the Streets**:

-   **The Residential Building**: Player housing (future). For now: a visible facade with a "coming soon" locked entrance. Balconies with NPC life visible above. Located off a side street, not directly on the plaza — housing is residential, not commercial.
-   **Bar/Social Venue**: An interior space for future social features. For now: visible exterior with music and light spilling out.
-   **Specialty Vendors**: Scattered along side streets. Lore-appropriate shops (arcanotechnic repair, clothing, stim vendors).

### Traversal (Lower)

-   Public lift arrives at a clearly marked landing area, ~30 seconds walk to the central plaza
-   Street layout encourages exploration but main paths are obvious
-   Walking time from lift landing to central plaza: ~20 seconds
-   Walking time across the full street network: ~2 minutes
-   Alleys offer shortcuts for players who learn the layout

---

## Vertical Connection — The Public Lift

**Location**: Edge of the upper plaza, prominent and visible from most of the upper level.

**Design**: Open platform (not an enclosed elevator). Players stand on it, see the city around them during the descent/ascent. The ride takes ~5-8 seconds — long enough to feel the transition, short enough to not annoy.

**Visual Transition**: As the lift descends, the open sky gives way to enclosing architecture. The color temperature shifts from cool (upper) to warm (lower). The ambient soundscape changes from quiet/wind to urban buzz.

**Capacity**: Multiple players simultaneously. No queue.

## Gameplay Flow

### First Visit

1. Player spawns on the upper level, near the military building
2. Natural pull toward the building entrance (it's the biggest landmark)
3. Explore lobby, find the briefing room (mission board), discover the armory
4. Exit, explore the gardens, find the perimeter views
5. Discover the public lift, descend to the lower level
6. Arrive in the streets, find the central plaza
7. Explore side streets, discover vendors and venues

### Returning Player (Daily Loop)

1. Spawn on upper level
2. Beeline to military building — check missions, gear up
3. Group up (in building or gardens)
4. Queue/launch mission from the lift
5. Post-mission: optionally descend to lower level for vendors, socializing
6. Log off wherever

### Social Player

1. Spawn, head to lower level
2. Hang out at central plaza, watch e-sports screens
3. Browse vendors on side streets
4. Meet up with friends
5. Group forms, head back up to military building to launch

## Phase 0 Scope

For Phase 0, the Prime Hub replaces the current CSG hub room. Minimum viable:

-   **Upper level**: Military building exterior (blockout), lobby with lift, one room (briefing). Garden area with basic geometry and a few props. Public lift to lower level.
-   **Lower level**: Central plaza with placeholder stalls. 2-3 connected streets. Residential building facade (locked).
-   **The public lift**: Functional, animated, handles the visual transition.
-   **NPCs**: Static placeholder NPCs in key locations (vendor spots, building reception).

NOT in Phase 0: Player housing, functional vendors, interior bar, specialty shops, e-sport screen content, seasonal events.

## Navigation Landmarks

Players should always know where they are. Each area needs a distinct visual anchor:

| Area                      | Landmark                            | Why It Works                                              |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Upper — Military Building | The building itself                 | Largest structure, visible from everywhere on upper level |
| Upper — Gardens           | The perimeter view (night sky)      | Unique — only place with an unobstructed sky view         |
| Upper — Lift              | Lit platform at plaza edge          | Glowing, mechanical, distinct from organic gardens        |
| Lower — Central Plaza     | Central fountain/monument           | Classic MMO waypoint, visible from connecting streets     |
| Lower — Residential       | Building facade with balcony lights | Tallest structure on lower level, warm glow               |
| Lower — Streets           | Holographic signage color-coding    | Different street clusters use different sign colors       |
