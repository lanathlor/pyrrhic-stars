# Prime Hub — Art Direction

## Empire Design Language

**The Empire builds in white.** Clean, bright, high-value surfaces. Think Apple Store meets military operations center. The architectural language is confident, well-funded, utopian — this is a post-scarcity civilization that can afford to make things beautiful.

**NOT dark, moody, or grimy.** The hub is a well-lit facility, not a space station corridor or dungeon.

## Reference Mood

**Military building (interior)**: Bright white/light grey walls, clean overhead panel lighting, polished floors. Blue holographic displays as accent color only. Reference: modern military command centers, clean sci-fi interiors (Star Trek, Bastion Orion concept art).

**Upper level (exterior)**: Open-air plaza, daylight or twilight sky, white/grey architecture with green gardens. Water features, mature trees. The "campus" feel of a tech company HQ.

**Lower level**: Dense vertical neighborhood, warm and buzzing. Warmer palette here — the contrast with the clean upper level is intentional. Street-level warmth vs. institutional cleanliness above.

The hub is **not** a battlefield, a dungeon, or a social lobby. It's a place where soldiers live between deployments. It should feel like home.

## Color & Lighting

### Military Building (Interior)

-   **Primary light source**: Overhead panel lights — bright neutral white (~5500K), evenly distributed
-   **Surface colors**: Walls off-white/light grey (0.8-0.85 value), floors medium grey (0.6), ceiling near-white (0.88-0.9)
-   **Accent**: Blue mana crystal trim strips — LOW energy, subtle glow at floor/ceiling edges. These are detail, not illumination.
-   **Holographic displays**: Blue-cyan emissive screens on consoles — the only saturated color in the room
-   **Overall palette**: White, light grey, medium grey. Blue as punctuation only.

### Upper Level (Open Sky)

-   **Primary light source**: Sky — twilight or daytime. Bright, open.
-   **Architecture**: White/light grey building facades, clean geometric lines
-   **Garden lighting**: Warm amber ground lights at night, sunlight during day
-   **Accent**: Greenery provides the color contrast — gardens against white architecture
-   **Overall palette**: White architecture, green gardens, blue sky. Clean and bright.

### Lower Level (Enclosed Streets)

-   **Primary light source**: Arcanotechnic signage — holographic shop signs, vendor displays, e-sport screens
-   **Street lighting**: Mana crystal lamp posts (warmer white than upper level, ~3500K equivalent)
-   **Ambient warmth**: Interior light spilling from apartment windows and storefronts (warm yellow-orange)
-   **Accent**: Chromatic shifts from NPC adaptive clothing, holographic ad displays cycling colors
-   **Overall palette**: Warm oranges and yellows (street level), cool blue-purple (distant upper structure visible overhead), saturated color pops from signage

### Lighting Principles

-   **No global ambient**: Every light has a visible source. If something is lit, the player can see what's lighting it.
-   **Contrast matters**: Dark corners exist. Not every alley is lit. This creates visual depth and guides the player's eye.
-   **Upper = cool dominant, lower = warm dominant**: The temperature shift when taking the lift should be immediately felt.

## Material Language

### Architecture

-   **Military building (upper)**: White composite panels, light grey metal accents, clean geometric surfaces. Polished and professional. Flat surfaces, sharp angles, recessed lighting channels. Think: modern government building, not industrial bunker.
-   **Garden structures (upper)**: Same concrete base, but weathered slightly. Planters are integrated into the architecture, not placed on top of it. The building grows into the garden.
-   **Street buildings (lower)**: Lighter materials — composite panels in muted tones (off-white, grey-blue, warm grey). More variation between buildings. Some have visible patching or modifications (residents personalizing their space). Balconies, awnings, hanging planters.
-   **Plaza surfaces (lower)**: Polished stone-analog flooring with embedded mana crystal guide lines. Slightly reflective when wet (it rains sometimes in vertical cities — condensation from upper levels).

### Arcanotechnic Elements

Mana crystals are **infrastructure, not decoration**. They should feel like LED strips or fiber optics in modern architecture — functional, integrated, and unremarkable to the inhabitants.

-   **Form**: Small crystalline elements housed in metal fixtures. Not raw floating crystals. Think: a glowing element behind frosted glass in a lamp housing.
-   **Glow behavior**: Soft, steady inner glow for infrastructure (lighting, door mechanisms, lift systems). Subtle pulse only for active systems (opening doors, moving lifts, active terminals).
-   **Color**: Default infrastructure is blue-white. Warmer tones (amber, soft orange) for residential/commercial areas. Red only for warnings or restricted zones.
-   **Scale**: Thumbnail-sized for light fixtures. Palm-sized for major infrastructure nodes (lift mechanism, building power junction). Players should not notice most of them consciously.

### Holographic Displays

-   **Fidelity**: Slightly noisy, translucent, with soft edge blur. Not razor-sharp Apple-style holograms. These are public municipal displays, not military-grade.
-   **Content**: Directory signs, shop names, transit schedules, e-sport match feeds, weather/condensation warnings.
-   **Behavior**: Slight flicker under stress (nearby combat would disrupt them). Subtle parallax shift as player moves past.

## Prop & Detail Guidelines

### Upper Level

-   Military checkpoint barriers (retracted in peacetime — visible but non-blocking)
-   Benches with integrated mana crystal reading lights
-   Water features: shallow channels or small fountains with soft underglow
-   Trees — real, tall, mature. Not saplings. These gardens are old.
-   Ground-cover plants, moss on older concrete edges
-   A few automated maintenance drones (small, non-threatening, background movement)

### Lower Level

-   Food stall carts with holographic menus
-   Seating clusters (mismatched chairs, low tables — organic gathering)
-   E-sport viewing screens (large, wall-mounted, showing match replays)
-   Street-level vendor displays (goods behind glass or hovering on small platforms)
-   Residential details: laundry on balconies, plant pots on windowsills, curtains with warm light behind
-   Public transit signage pointing to maglev lines (non-functional in-game, but present for worldbuilding)
-   Small shrine/memorial corners (the war has been going for 20+ years — people remember)

### NPCs & Crowd

-   Majority in functional, comfortable clothing — soft fabrics, muted colors, practical cuts
-   Some in military uniform (off-duty, but identifiable)
-   Wealthy individuals: adaptive clothing with subtle luminous effects (chromatic shifts, texture changes)
-   Counterculture types: tattoos, asymmetric cuts, dissent symbols (stylistic, not aggressive)
-   Exoskeleton-assisted veterans (visible mechanical supports, normalized)
-   Nobody looks hungry, homeless, or desperate. This is post-scarcity. Density is cultural, not poverty.

## What to Avoid

| Do NOT                                              | Instead                                                                       |
| --------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Sterile white corridors (Mass Effect Citadel)       | Lived-in spaces with wear and personalization                                 |
| Grimy cyberpunk poverty (Cyberpunk 2077 slums)      | Clean density — packed but comfortable                                        |
| Medieval fantasy anything (stone, torches, banners) | Sci-fi materials, arcanotechnic lighting                                      |
| Raw floating magic crystals                         | Crystals housed in fixtures, integrated into infrastructure                   |
| Over-the-top military propaganda                    | Subtle imperial presence — flags exist, but this isn't North Korea            |
| Empty spaces                                        | Fill with life details — even unused corners should suggest someone uses them |
| Uniform NPCs                                        | Variety in age, style, body type, mobility aids                               |

## Reference Touchstones

-   **Blade Runner 2049** (city interiors): warm/cool contrast, lived-in density, large-scale architecture
-   **Ghost in the Shell (1995)** (market scenes): layered vertical city, warm neon-analog lighting
-   **The Expanse** (Ceres station interiors): functional military/civilian hybrid, cramped but not squalid
-   **Final Fantasy VII Remake** (Sector 7 slums reimagined): a dense neighborhood that feels like home despite the megastructure above
-   **Mirror's Edge** (upper areas only): clean architectural lines, bold color accents against neutral surfaces
